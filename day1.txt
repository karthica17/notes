Today is the foundation day. If you understand Day 1 properly, the rest of DevOps will make much more sense.

1. Beginner Explanation

Before understanding DevOps, let's understand how software is built.

Suppose Swiggy wants to introduce a new feature:

"Live Order Tracking"

Can a developer directly write code and make it available to all users?

No.

There is a process followed by every company.

This process is called SDLC (Software Development Life Cycle).
  
Requirement
    ↓
Design
    ↓
Development
    ↓
Testing
    ↓
Deployment
    ↓
Maintenance

DevOps helps automate and speed up this entire process.

2. Why It Is Needed

      ↓

Agile & Scrum
What is Agile?

Agile is a way of developing software in small pieces instead of building everything at once.

Old Method:

Collect Requirements
      ↓
Develop for 1 Year
      ↓
Release

Problem:

Users wait too long.

Agile Method
Feature 1
   ↓
Release

Feature 2
   ↓
Release

Feature 3
   ↓
Release

Users get features faster.

What is Scrum?

Scrum is a framework used to implement Agile.

A team works in short cycles called Sprints.

Usually:

Sprint = 2 Weeks

Example:

Sprint 1:

Login Feature

Sprint 2:

Payment Feature

Sprint 3:

Notifications
Real Industry Example
Amazon

Amazon doesn't wait 6 months to release features.

Instead:

Small Change
     ↓
Testing
     ↓
Deployment

Thousands of small deployments happen every day.

DevOps makes this possible.
